Summary

There are many different kinds of frauds, but all of them don't have good intentions for the victims, most of the deceivers want money or personal information. So they act like they have good intentions, by saying they themselves are a victim of something or by tricking some poor souls into giving them money for some kind of deal. These deals are most to good to be true like just giving away a few million dollars or other currencies and real dangerous one are the ones that could seem reasonable like a friendly charity, which sets sail to the some tropical island after some time.

Charity fraud

Like above descripted a Charity Fraud is made by a charity which deceives user of a website or other service so they give some money to an supposed charity.

These scamming charities are not to different from the a bit less malicious charities which only put a percentage of the profit into their own bags. While those are quite unmoral as well they at least give most of the collected money to the cause the user thought got their money.

It is pretty hard to spot this kind of fraud, because they mostly seem like a real one. But if the charity you want to spend your money one does one of these things:

  • no regular updates how the money is spent
  • there aren't any indications about who the site owner is
  • there isn't much information about the cause
  • there have been incidents

You should probably think about your actions twice because else your money might be the funds of the next vacation of some guy pretending to be a charity

Gambling Fraud

Much like charity fraud this kind of scheme can be pretty hard to spot, because there is a chance that you loose your money when you are gambling even though you gamble on a pretty respected site.

Some people might argue that every kind of gambling is some kind of fraud but we don't want to debate about this topic. With this spirit we declare every kind of gambling where the participants don't have any chance to win something or even break even a fraud.

The operators of the casino can trick people to get money through rigging the game or getting other associated people to encourage them to bet more or just straight up don't give them any chance of winning.

to spot such a scheme your best option is to check their website for inconsistencies or other sketchy stuff. Other than that its again up to your gut to decide if you can trust the services. Of course number one rule is if an offer feels too good to be true it's probably not true and just some kind of scheme.

email schemes

Such schemes are probably already well known to most people already were in such an situation or heard stories from other people. For anybody who doesn't know the scheme thats described above here's a quick explanation. The suspect sends an emotional e-mail to victims, which e-mail addresses they got from some database, in this e-mail the is some story like an relative died and to inherit their belongings you should pay a fee. It could also be that there is an offer where you pay a fee and get money somehow but it could also "escalate" into a charity frauds through a heart warming story about a cancer patient or anything else like that.

Most of these scammers probably just want to get some easy bucks from you but some are also out for personal information or credit card details

Pyramid Scheme

A pyramid scheme is more like a really sketchy company that tries to communicate to people that they can make easy money just by paying a fee and than promoting this "great" company with its unbelievable deals to get more people into signing in. A victim may actually get some kind of profit, but because the only thing the company does is hiring people for a fee and get them to sign even more people into this rabbit hole. So the only income is the fee everybody has to pay.

As you may already thought the income the employees get are the fees of the victims below. Because of this model you always need to get more subjects into this mess to get money for the other poor souls who signed into this. The only person who makes a proper profit is the person who started the whole thing. Most of the time he will just get out of the company with all the earnings and the victims who probably didn't have much money before have just invested into one of the most unethical ways to make some quick cash.

The legal situation about this scheme is quite weird, because if you get people invest their money into this scheme it's illegal, but if you get people to buy a product as the fee it's legal in most countries. This product is practically something that can be produced or bought dirt cheap

Tech support

This kind of scheme is own where a supposed techsupport employee that tries to get its victims money, data or just to put on some malware. Such an operation can be achieved in many ways.

The most common method is to get some users through sketchy ads on some websites to call a well known company(of course its their number), because the user is infected with some kind of malware. This websites are most of the time bootleg copies of the official website, but the domain needs to be different so you watch out for this. If you phone them they will probably try to sell you some overpriced product for an nonexistent thread. This software could also be some kind of malware.

To convince you that you are indeed hacked by somebody, they interpret the netstat command wrong or just type in a message while a tree command is running. They often use screen sharing software like team viewer for this.
