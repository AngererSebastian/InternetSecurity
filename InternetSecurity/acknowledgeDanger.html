Introduction

This part of the web page will show how to not be fooled into some kind of malware or scheme. It accomplishes that through telling you what to look out for and well it also will talk about that you shouldn't trust anyone unless they have proven themselves trustworthy.

Common Sense

The first thing to avoid getting into frauds is to use common sense. For example if somebody wants to give you money just out of the kind of their heart it is probably not trustworthy and you should just move along. A goood way of thinking in these kind of situation is what the other person could get from you. It could be anything from public reputation to some kind of income on their behalf.

Another thing that falls other common sense is to be skeptical of strangers. And even if a suppossed famous person is making an offer to you think about the situation rationally and never truly believe them until the have proven that they aren't some kind of pretender.

Searching

If for example an unknown company makes you an offer, you should probably feel like you have been approached by a stranger on the street that has made you an unbelievable offer. Just because there isn't just one shady person behind doesn't mean you are safe. So you should search up the company's history(on their website, and other sources as well) if there are any contradicting facts it's probably safest to decline the offer. Even if there is a well known and trustworthy company behind. Check if their e-mail addresses, domain names,... match up with the originals on the company's website. This could also that the pretending website uses an old theme of the original or other small details which don't make sense.

To check if some company is trustworthy you can also try to ask the employees details about their company and the supposed location(more useful if you live in the area) this can be easiest accomplished during an tech support or e-mail scheme, because you are in direct contact with the employees and the company probably doesn't really operate under the described condition, unlike a pyramid scheme where the company really exists, but the fact that you probably won't get any money from this isn't said.

Think about it

This the part where you gathered all important information and just need to think about the whole situation this is the most tricky part of the whole process because just need to think about what you found if it is important. If you found anything of significant value or situation wasn't important to you at all it's pretty easy to get to an opinion, but if you didn't find any strong evidence and liked the offer or whatever it is pretty hard to get to the point. You could try to sleep over it, if you can't find one valid opinion. Then the best option should be clear, at least we hope so. But as you already noticed it comes all down to your own gut to decide if something is malicious or not.
